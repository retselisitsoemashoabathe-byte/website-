Traditional Dances

Mokhibo

women's dance performed kneeling,
using graceful arm and shoulder movements to music and chants.

Mohobelo

A male-only dance showcasing stamping, jumping,
and traditional chants. It expresses strength and unity.

Setapo

A popular dance among women.
Focuses on rhythm created by shaking shoulders and feet movements.

sebekere

A social dance performed in villages during gatherings.
Involves circular group formations, clapping, and singing

Instruments

likupu

are made of different vessels,
for the purpose of making sounds

Lesiba

A stringed wind instrument
played by blowing into a string tied
to a flexible stick. Unique to the Basotho.

Thomo

A mouth bow instrument used by women to
produce rhythmic and melodic sounds.

Setolo-tolo

A jaw harp-like instrument that mimics vocal rhythms
using metal and oral resonance.

Kalimba

In Sesotho, people sometimes call it a
“piano ea lehong” (literally wooden piano)
when describing it, but it’s not originally
a deeply rooted Basotho instrument—it’s more widespread in other Bantu cultures.

lesira

Similar to the sekhankula and lesiba,
but the sound comes from plucking
the string with the fingers instead of blowing.

koto

It’s used in traditional songs, dances,
and storytelling — mostly in rural settings.
It can be played by plucking or striking the string
